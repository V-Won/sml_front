Privacy Policy

By Article 30 of the Personal Information Protection Act, Samkwang Medical Lab has established and disclosed the following personal information processing policy to protect the privacy rights and interests of information subjects and to smoothly handle user grievances related to personal information.

Article 1. Purpose of Processing Personal Information
The Company processes personal information for the following purposes. Processed personal information will not be used for purposes other than the specified ones, and in cases where the purpose of use changes, necessary measures, including obtaining separate consent in accordance with Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act, will be taken.
  • Utilization for identity verification procedures related to diagnostic testing and membership service usage.
  • Ensuring smooth communication channels for notification, handling complaints, and providing information on new services and events.
  • Provision of differentiated medical content.
  • Dispatch of certificates and delivery of goods.
  • Compilation of data for the development of new services and provision of personalized services.
  • Provision of medical services, billing, collection, and reimbursement services for examinations, diagnoses, and treatments.
  • Educational and research activities as a designated hospital for education and research.
  • Basic data for external entrusted diagnostic testing and clinical trial audits.
  • Provision of health content and clinical research information.
  • Sending medical expense statements, certificates, and dispatching items related to examinations and tests.
  • Collection of information for consumers in accordance with Article 52 of the Consumer Basic Act.
Article 2. Processing and Retention Period of Personal Information
The Company will promptly destroy your personal information when the purpose of collection or provision has been achieved.
  • In the case of membership registration information: When you withdraw from membership or are expelled from membership.
  • In the case of information collected for surveys, events, etc.: When the respective survey or event has concluded.
  • In the case of information collected for the provision of inspections and medical services: Retained in accordance with the standards specified in medical law (5 years). However, even when the purpose of collection or provision has been achieved, your personal information may be retained if there is a need to do so under the provisions of relevant laws such as the Commercial Act and the Medical Service Act.
Article 3. Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties
The Company, except with your consent or as required by relevant laws, will not use or provide your personal information beyond the scope notified in the 『Purpose of Processing Personal Information.』
However, exceptions are made in the following cases:
  • When users have given prior consent for disclosure.
  • When there is a request from investigative agencies in accordance with legal procedures and methods for investigation purposes, as stipulated by law.
  • When it is necessary for statistical, academic research, or market research purposes, and the information is provided in a form that cannot identify specific individuals.
Article 4. Matters Regarding the Outsourcing of Personal Information
The Company, with your consent, entrusts specific tasks to third parties. Only the minimum necessary personal information is provided in accordance with relevant laws. The Company manages, supervises, and ensures the secure handling of personal information. Please refer to the [List of Personal Information Outsourcing Companies] for information on tasks and companies related to outsourcing.
Article 5. Rights and Obligations of Data Subjects and Legal Representatives and the Method of Exercising Them
5.1 Rights and method of exercise for users under 14 years of age and their legal representatives:
Membership registration for users under 14 years of age (hereinafter referred to as "children") requires the consent of their legal representatives when collecting personal information. Legal representatives of children can exercise rights such as access, correction, deletion, and processing suspension regarding the child's personal information.
5.2 Method of Withdrawal of Consent / Membership Withdrawal:
You can withdraw your consent for the collection, use, and provision of personal information at any time. Membership withdrawal can be done by contacting the personal information protection officer or relevant department in writing, by phone, or fax. Your personal information will be promptly disposed of.
5.3 Matters related to the installation/operation of automatic collection devices for personal information and refusal of such:
The Company automatically logs information when using the website for the following purposes
  • Analyzing the frequency and visiting time of members and non-members to understand user preferences and areas of interest for service improvement.
  • Utilizing traces of viewed web pages and visited pages to provide personalized services during the next visit.
  • Understanding the level of participation and visit frequency in various events and surveys conducted by the Company to provide differentiated opportunities and information based on individual interests.
  • Recording logs for legal compliance and security measures.
Article 6. Items of Personal Information Processed
The Company collects the minimum necessary personal information to conduct tests entrusted by medical institutions and provide test results.
6.1 Items collected during reference laboratory diagnostic testing:
  • Required items: Name, gender, date of birth.
  • Optional items: Chart number, age, phone number, address, mobile phone number, email, and other information necessary for tests.
6.2 Items necessary for the operation of the website:
Information automatically generated during the process of using website services, such as service usage records, access logs, cookies, and IP information.
Article 7. Procedure and Method for the Destruction of Personal Information
Personal information is promptly destroyed when the retention period expires, the processing purpose is achieved, or when personal information becomes unnecessary. If personal information must be retained under other laws, it is stored separately.
The procedure, period, and method of destruction are as follows:
7.1. Destruction Procedure
Unnecessary personal information and personal information files are handled according to internal policy procedures under the responsibility of the Personal Information Protection Officer. Personal information that has exceeded the retention period is promptly destroyed from the date of expiration.
Destruction of Personal Information Files:
  • When the processing objectives of personal information files are achieved, the relevant service is terminated, or the business is concluded.
  • When a personal information file is deemed unnecessary, it is promptly destroyed from the date it is recognized as no longer required for processing.
7.2. Destruction Method
  • Information in electronic form is destroyed using technical methods that render the records irretrievable.
  • Personal information printed on paper is destroyed either by shredding or through incineration.
Article 8. Installation, Operation, and Refusal of Automatic Collection Devices for Personal Information
To use the Company's services, mandatory and optional items must be provided during registration. However, for optional items such as email reception preferences, service usage is not restricted if left blank. Additionally, users can allow, confirm, or refuse cookies by adjusting their web browser options without affecting service usage. Cookies are small pieces of information sent by the server (http) operating the website to the user's computer browser and may be stored on the user's PC hard drive.
8.1. Purpose of Using Cookies
Cookies are used to understand the visit and usage patterns of users on various services and websites to provide optimized information.
8.2. Rejecting Cookie Settings
Users can refuse cookie storage by turning off the option to allow sites to save and read cookie data in the web browser settings. For example, in Microsoft Edge, users can go to Settings > Cookies and Site Permissions and turn off the option to allow sites to store and read cookie data.
Article 9. Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Information
9.1. Establishment and Implementation of Internal Management Plans
The Company establishes and implements internal management plans for personal information protection annually.
9.2. Minimization and Education of Personnel Handling Personal Information
The Company designates personnel to handle personal information and implements measures to minimize and manage personal information.
9.3. Access Restriction to Personal Information
Access rights to databases handling personal information are granted, modified, and revoked to control access to personal information. Intrusion prevention systems are used to control unauthorized access from external sources.
9.4. Preservation and Prevention of Tampering with Access Records
Access records (web logs, summary information, etc.) are stored and managed for a minimum of six months. Security features are used to prevent tampering, theft, or loss of access records.
9.5. Encryption of Personal Information
User's personal information is stored and managed in an encrypted form. Additionally, important data is encrypted during storage and transmission using separate security measures.
9.6. Technical Measures for Hacking Prevention
Security programs are installed, regularly updated, and checked to prevent personal information leakage or damage caused by hacking, computer viruses, etc. Restricted areas with controlled access are established, and technical and physical monitoring and blocking are implemented to detect and prevent attempts to change information illegally.
9.7. Access Control for Unauthorized Persons
Physical storage locations of personal information systems are separated, and access control procedures are established and operated to restrict access.
